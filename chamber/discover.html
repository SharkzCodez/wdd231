History of Alice

Alice was originally a railway depot in 1883 called "Bandana" that experienced an economic boost through cattle shipment. When applying for a post office, the plan was to name the town "Kleberg" after Robert Justus Kleberg, however, there was another town named Kleberg already. The town was instead named "Alice" after Alice Gertrudis King Kleberg, Robert Justus Kleberg Jr.'s wife and the daughter of Richard and Henrietta King. In the 1940s, petroleum was found underneath the town, causing a slight population boom.

Alice Culture

While Alice is a small town, it had a big cultural impact on Tejano music, being called the "Birthplace of Tejano Music" despite its origins in Northern Mexico. The first recording company to produce exclusively Tejano music, Ideal Records, was based in Alice and helped spread the genre of music to a wider audience. Alice is also the birthplace of two Nobel Prize winners, Robert F. Curl Jr. and James P. Allison who won for chemistry and medicine respectively.

Activities in Alice

Jim Wells County Fair - This annual celebration features local crafts, food trucks and stands, and a carnival during the month of October.

Historic Downtown - Alice's downtown features vintage boutiques, resturants, and other stores that make you feel like you are standing in another time.

Alice Natatorium - The local YMCA features both an indoor pool and a outdoor waterpark with slides and a lazy river that opens in the summer.

South Texas Museum - The museum boasts the judge's bench from the Box 13 Scandal case as well as artifacts like old railway lamps and a horse collar woven from corn shucks.

Lake Findley - Due to a wide variety of hides and depths in the shallow lake, Lake Findley provides an area for assorted fish species as well as birds who come to feast on the aquatic life.

Alice Farmer's Market - The Farmer's Market boasts jellies and preserves, fresh eggs and meat, honey, baked goods, jewelry, and other assorted crafts.
